Pellow is an interactive installation that reveals what happens under the hood of a browser when you visit web pages, click on links or search for specific content.

You can navigate any content with the Google Chrome web browser. Every time you access a page, click a link, or view online content, Pellow reveals the different organizations that serve content to the browser, as well as the different locations that are accessed. The Pellow installation also emphasizes the size of the different pages and sites that you visit. This size depends on the amount of images, videos or interactivity that is present in the page.

Tekniska
This installation is named after Nicola Pellow. She is an Internet pionner who programmed the second web browser ever created, in 1991: the Line Mode Browser.
Pellow is software art installation designed and developed by the rethread.art collective. The collective is supported by the KTH Royal Institute of Technology, the KMH Royal College of Music, the CASTOR center for software research and the Wallenberg Autonomous Systems, Software and AI Program (WASP).
